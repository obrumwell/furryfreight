Our Story

Just by changing their location, pets go from unwanted and discarded to loved members of the family.

How It Started

One road trip changed everything.

Furry Freight was founded in 2018 by Kim Wilken, a registered nurse living in Bend, Oregon.

The solution seemed straightforward. Drive the animals from where they had no chance, to where families were waiting. That first road trip became the foundation of what is now Furry Freight Shelter Transport.

We are a fully volunteer-run 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Every dollar donated goes directly to the animals in need.

Support Our Work Our Programs
Our Mission

Reducing euthanasia of adoptable pets, one road trip at a time.

We partner with shelters in overpopulated areas where pets face euthanasia not because they are unadoptable due to health or behavior, but because there are simply too many of them and not enough adopters, or space in the shelter. We transport those shelter pets to our Pacific Northwest rescue partners and soon after, loving forever homes.

Just by changing their location, pets go from overlooked and at risk to adopted and adored.

What We Believe

Our guiding principles

Every life has value

No healthy, adoptable animal should lose its life because of where it was born. Geography should not determine fate.

Community solves problems

Shelters, volunteers, donors, and adopters working together accomplish what none could do alone. We are stronger as a network.

Prevention matters too

Transport saves lives today. Our Spay it Forward program works to reduce the number of animals entering shelters tomorrow.

Leadership

Meet the board

Kim Wilken

Founder & Board Member

A registered nurse in Bend, Oregon, Kim founded Furry Freight after seeing the disparity between overcrowded shelters in other states and adoption demand in the Pacific Northwest. She has spent years volunteering at rescues, shelters, and spay/neuter clinics. Avid hiker, paddleboarder, and devoted dog mom.

Olivia Brumwell

Board Member

Born and raised in Bend, Olivia volunteered with Furry Freight for three years before joining the board. She brings a background as a veterinary assistant and serves as Director of Operations at a youth nonprofit. Mom to rescue dogs Harvey and Aero. Loves rock climbing, vintage furniture, and gardening.

Patty Baragona

Board Member

With over 20 years in bookkeeping and office management, Patty retired to Bend in 2014 and joined Furry Freight in 2024. She brings sharp organizational skills and a big heart. Pickleball lover and proud mom to rescue pup Lucy.
