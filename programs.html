What We Do

Our Programs

Two ways we fight for adoptable animals. Moving them to safety today, and reducing the number who need saving tomorrow.

Program 1

Transport

Shelters in some parts of the country are overwhelmed. They take in more animals than their communities can adopt, and despite the best efforts of their staff, healthy and adoptable dogs and cats run out of time.

In the Pacific Northwest the picture looks different. Shelters have capacity. Adopters are waiting. The demand for pets is high and the supply is low.

Furry Freight bridges that gap. We partner with high-intake shelters in overpopulated areas and transport their at-risk animals directly to Pacific Northwest partner shelters where they are quickly placed into loving homes.

Research shows that transport is one of the most effective tools available for reducing shelter euthanasia rates. It addresses the imbalance directly, moving animals from where there are too many to where there are not enough.

How Transport Works

From at-risk to adopted

Every transport follows a careful process to keep animals safe and get them to their new homes as quickly as possible.

1

Partner shelters identify animals at risk

Our partner shelters in overpopulated areas flag dogs and cats who are healthy and adoptable but facing euthanasia due to limited space or low local demand.

2

Animals are vetted and health-checked

Before travel, all animals receive a health check and any required vaccinations or documentation for interstate transport.

3

Volunteer drivers hit the road

Our trained volunteer drivers take multi-day road trips, transporting animals safely in pairs. Drivers switch off to keep animals moving and stress levels low.

4

Animals arrive at Pacific Northwest partners

Partner shelters in Oregon and surrounding states receive the animals, provide any additional care needed, and begin the adoption process.

5

Animals find their forever homes

Thanks to high demand in the Pacific Northwest, animals transported by Furry Freight are typically adopted quickly after arrival.

Program 2

Spay it Forward

Transport saves lives today. But the shelters we partner with keep filling up because too many animals are entering the system to begin with. Spay it Forward is our answer to that problem.

Launched in December 2021, this program funds spay and neuter surgeries for pets whose owners want to do the right thing but cannot afford the cost. Our first dog was fixed in Tulsa, Oklahoma, at no cost to the owner. Since then, we have set up regular surgery appointments through our rescue partners and are working to expand to more clinics.

We focus on large breed dogs and cats, which are statistically the hardest to adopt out and the most at risk of euthanasia in overcrowded shelters.

Each surgery costs about $70. To sponsor one, donate any amount and write "Spay it Forward" in the comments. Every surgery prevents dozens of future at-risk animals.
